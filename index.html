Our Story

Embusuro Yabene, Women Group

We are women led and run community medical center that supports people living with disability especially the deaf, marginalized and vulnerable communities in Kisii county. We provide customized services to deaf community. We provide the following services to the PLWDs with special focus on women.

  • Communication interpretation on sign language to enable PlWDs access basic medical services.
  • Create awareness on primary health care especially on communicable and lifestyle diseases.
  • Capacity building among health facilities on disability mainstream.
  • Training of community health promoters on preventive health care.
  • Nutritional support and campaign against lifestyle diseases.
  • Physical health education among learning institutions, communities and households.
  • Partnerships with NCPWDs, governments and partners to provide community health services.
  • Provision of palliative care services to those at household suffering from terminal illness e.g. cancer, hepatitis
  • Agricultural innovations for self reliance, farm produce aggregation, trade and marketing
Our Mission

"To provide holistic and inclusive healthcare, agricultural empowerment, and support services to people living with disabilities, as well as marginalized and vulnerable populations in Kisii County. Our mission is to foster self-reliance, inclusivity, and well-being by delivering specialized care, innovative agricultural solutions, and educational programs, thereby creating a more equitable and resilient society."

Our Vision

"Our vision is to be a leading force in Kisii County for healthcare, agricultural innovation, and community support. We aspire to create a future where all individuals, regardless of their abilities or circumstances, have equal access to quality healthcare and the opportunity to achieve self-sufficiency. We envision a society where people living with disabilities can lead fulfilling lives, realizing their potential and contributing to the well-being of the community. By forging partnerships, raising awareness, and championing inclusivity, we aim to be a catalyst for positive change, inspiring a more compassionate, accessible, and empowered world."

Our Causes

Healthcare Services

Communication barriers: People with disabilities, especially the deaf, face barriers in accessing healthcare due to communication challenges and lack of specialized care.

Sign Language Awareness in Hospitals: Deaf individuals encounter communication barriers when seeking medical care, leading to misdiagnosis and inadequate treatment.

Palliative Care: Those with chronic or terminal illnesses may suffer from pain, distress, and poor quality of life.

Awareness on Communicable and Lifestyle Diseases: Lack of awareness and education about diseases can result in a higher prevalence of communicable and lifestyle diseases.

Agriculture

Agricultural Innovation for Self-Reliance: Individuals with disabilities often face economic and social exclusion, as they may struggle to find employment or engage in income-generating activities.

Farm Produce and Marketing: Many individuals with disabilities may have difficulty marketing their agricultural products and accessing profitable markets.

Social Development

Supporting Marginalized Communities: Marginalized and vulnerable communities often lack access to essential services, leading to poor health and socio-economic outcomes.

Partnerships with Other Agencies and Government Institutions: Limited resources and fragmented efforts can hinder the effectiveness of community support initiatives.
